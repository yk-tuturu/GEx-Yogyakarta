The final class of gamelan instruments are the punctuating instruments. 
These include various types of gongs, as well as drum instruments such as the kendang. 
The kendang is especially notable for its role in guiding the whole ensemble, providing a rhythmic base to guide other instruments through tempo changes, starts and stops. 
Not only would its player need a strong understanding of rhythm, but also good coordination to strike the kendang at specific angles to produce different inflections and beats. 
Its sound is not very prominent or loud, yet they play a vital role in supporting the whole ensemble.
