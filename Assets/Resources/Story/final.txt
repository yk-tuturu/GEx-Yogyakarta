Lagu Gugur Gunung is a folk song about <i>gotong-royong</i>. 
Roughly translated, gotong-royong means to communally work together and mutually provide aid to those around us. 
Be it within our social circle, our villages and community, and even for the country, 
the song emphasizes that anything can be achieved far more efficiently when everyone is willing to work together.
We have seen instances of this all throughout Yogyakarta
From ground-up initiatives and community centers designed to support local villages and communities, 
to artists collaborating with other artists to convey stronger messages--
The list goes on. 
Yet it seems like in more modernized cities such as Singapore, this sense of duty has largely been lost to a more single-minded pursuit of personal worth. 
Just as a gamelan requires layers and layers of instruments to display its true complexity and nuance, 
people truly flourish when they are able to work together in harmony. 
A saron's melody is too simple;
A bonang is too soft and mellow to shine on its own;
A kendang only provides the rhythm, but no substance. 
These simple percussive instruments only reach their true potential when played together in a group.
In this final level, we will put everything together to form the complete composition of Lagu Gugur Gunung.
