There is a second class of gamelan instruments known as the <b>elaborating</b> instruments. 
In Indonesian, these instruments can be called <i>pengisi</i>, which means to fill in the gaps. 
It is the role of the elaborating instruments to add movement and nuance to the basic melody provided by the balungan. 
Each instrument follows its own rules in its elaboration. 
For instance, the instrument for this level is the bonang barung, a set of bronze pots held in a wooden frame. 
The bonang is generally played on the off-beats of the main melody, presenting a unique rhythmic challenge to the player. 
The result, when combined with the main melody, is a 'fuller', more complete sound.
